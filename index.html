Executive Summary

The Doc Extraction Pilot achieved a 10.11% overall extraction rate across 300 extractions from 2,966 tasks. Performance shows significant variation across 11 participating law firms, with three high adopters driving the majority of success. Quality metrics are encouraging, with extraction errors at 5.0% compared to 6.4% for FileServe baseline.

Daily Trends

Larsen (62.02%): Started with very low counts at the pilot's launch, but by early August was posting 9–10 extractions per day. Clear upward trend and consistent retention.

Kasowitz (29.79%): Entered later (early August) but has maintained steady daily usage with no major gaps. Retention is stable since adoption.

Consumer Legal (21.58%): Began with a spike on 7/30 and has shown recurring activity through mid-August, including several multi-extraction days. Retained with a stable rhythm.

Rejection Analysis

Overall extraction error rate of 5.0% compares favorably to FileServe's 6.4% baseline. Error rates vary significantly by customer, from 0% (7 customers) to 12.5% (American Lemon Law Group). Top performers maintain acceptable error rates: Larsen 5.0%, Kasowitz 7.1%, Consumer Legal 7.3%.

Retention

Summary: All three top firms are more engaged now than at pilot start. Larsen shows the biggest growth trajectory, Kasowitz maintains steady engagement since entry, and Consumer Legal demonstrates consistent activity patterns.

The retention pattern indicates that firms who achieve initial success tend to maintain or increase their usage over time, suggesting strong product-market fit among high-adopting customers.

Narrative

The pilot reveals a concentrated but expanding adoption pattern with five firms (45% of participants) now above 10% extraction rate, demonstrating growing market validation. Three high adopters continue to drive the majority of success, but two mid-tier performers show promising engagement.

Concentration Risk: Without Larsen's exceptional performance, the extraction rate drops from 10.21% to 7.90%, showing continued dependency on the top performer but with improved baseline performance across the remaining customer base.
